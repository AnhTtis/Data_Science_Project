Explainable AI (XAI) has established itself as an important component of AI-driven interactive systems.
With Augmented Reality (AR) becoming more integrated in daily lives, the role of XAI also becomes essential in AR because end-users will frequently interact with intelligent services. 
However, it is unclear how to design effective XAI experiences for AR. 
We propose XAIR, a design framework that addresses \textit{when}, \textit{what}, and \textit{how} to provide explanations of AI output in AR.
The framework was based on a multi-disciplinary literature review of XAI and HCI research, a large-scale survey probing 500+ end-usersâ€™ preferences for AR-based explanations, and three workshops with 12 experts collecting their insights about XAI design in AR. 
XAIR's utility and effectiveness was verified via a study with 10 designers and another study with 12 end-users.
XAIR can provide guidelines for designers, inspiring them to identify new design opportunities and achieve effective XAI designs in AR.
