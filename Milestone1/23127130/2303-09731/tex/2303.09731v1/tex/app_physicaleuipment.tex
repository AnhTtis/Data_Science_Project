As shown in Fig.\ref{fig:appearing_attack_equipment}, a general physical equipment for appearing attacks consists of several photodiodes, a delay component and several combinations of laser transmitters and lens. Usually, the photodiode is responsible for beginning the attack. Once the photodiode receives the lasers emitted by the victim ADS's LiDAR, it generates the current and activates the delay component.
The delay component then controls the laser transmitter to emit lasers to the LiDAR and injects points into the PC generated by the LiDAR. Because the delay component can determine the time of flight of emitted lasers, the laser transmitter can determine the light intensity of emitted lasers, and the lens can determine the direction of emitted lasers, the attacker is possible to inject a certain number of points within a certain area as he/she wishes.
