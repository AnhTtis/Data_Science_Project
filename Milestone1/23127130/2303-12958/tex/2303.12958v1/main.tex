\title{On the Deformation Theory of $\bb{E}_{\infty}$-Coalgebras}
\author{Florian Riedel}
\begin{abstract}
  We introduce a notion of formally \'etale $\bb{E}_{\infty}$-coalgebras and show that these
  admit essentially unique, functorial lifts against square zero extensions of $\bb{E}_{\infty}$-rings.
  We use this to construct a spherical Witt vector style functor which exhibits
  the $\infty$-category of formally \'etale connective $\bb{E}_{\infty}$-coalgebras over $\F_{p}$
  as a full subcategory of the $\infty$-category of connective $p$-complete $\bb{E}_{\infty}$-coalgebras
  over $\S_{p}^{\wedge}$. Finally, we prove that for a finite space $X$ the $\F_{p}$-homology
  $C_{\pt}(X;\F_{p})$ is a formally \'etale $\F_{p}$-coalgebra and hence
  $(\Sigma^{\infty}_{+}X)^{\wedge}_{p}$ can be recovered as its essentially unique lift
 to the $p$-completed sphere.
\end{abstract}
