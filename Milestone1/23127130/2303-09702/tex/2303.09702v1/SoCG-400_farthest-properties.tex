\subparagraph*{Properties of Farthest Edges.}
\label{sec:farthest-properties}
We need the following basic ``triangle property'' (proved in Appendix~\ref{appendix:farthest-properties}) about shortest paths that cross. 

\begin{lem}
\label{lem:new_order}
Suppose the points $p$, $q$ and the edges $e$, $f$ occur in the order $p,q,e,f$ along the polygon boundary $\partial P$.
Then $d(p,e) + d(q,f) \geq d(p,f) + d(q,e)$.
\end{lem}

We then characterize what two paths to farthest edges are like, see Figure~\ref{fig:correct_orderings} and Appendix~\ref{appendix:farthest-properties}.  In particular, we generalize the farthest vertex Ordering Property~\cite{aronov1993farthest} as follows.


\smallskip\noindent
{\bf The Ordering Property.}
As $p$ moves clockwise around $\partial  P$, so does $F(p)$.
