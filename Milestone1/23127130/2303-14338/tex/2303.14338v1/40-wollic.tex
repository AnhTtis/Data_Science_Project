Whenever a belief transition depends on explanations, it has a self-confirming explanation. If an $X$-parametrized family of transitions is, say, in the form $h\colon \DP\otimes X\otimes A\to B$, then the prediction $h(p,x,a)$ can be steered by the explanations $p$ to confirm one of them, say $\Psi$, in the sense that $h(\Psi x, x, a) = \uev{\Psi x} a$. 

\begin{proposition}
For any belief transition $h \in \UUU(\DP\otimes X\otimes A, B)$ there is an explanation $\Psi\in \tot \UUU(X,\DP)$ such that
