\begin{abstract}

Data visualizations have been widely used on mobile devices like smartphones for various tasks (e.g., visualizing personal health and financial data), 
% enabling a convenient view of 
making it convenient for people to view
such data anytime and anywhere.
% and people can conveniently view such mobile data visualizations anytime and anywhere. 
However, others nearby can also easily peek at the visualizations, resulting in personal data disclosure. In this paper, we propose a perception-driven approach to transform mobile data visualizations into privacy-preserving ones.
% Inspired by prior studies on visual perception,
Specifically, based on human visual perception, we
% we investigate prior studies on visual perception, and further
% we 
develop a masking scheme to adjust the spatial frequency and luminance contrast of colored visualizations. 
% , which 
The resulting visualization retains its original 
% visualization 
information in close proximity but reduces visibility when viewed from a certain distance or farther away. We conducted two user studies to inform the design of our approach (N=16) and systematically evaluate its performance (N=18), respectively. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach in terms of privacy preservation for mobile data visualizations.
