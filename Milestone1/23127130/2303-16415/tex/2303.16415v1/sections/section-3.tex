%----------------------------------%
\section{Linear Stability Analysis}
\label{sec:3}
%----------------------------------%

In this section, we present the results of linear stability analysis. 
As mentioned earlier, we limit our analysis to $Pr=1$ and radius ratios $\eta=0.6, 0.9$ which correspond to wide--gap and thin--gap cases respectively. 
We sweep over the angular velocity ratio ($\mu$) and azimuthal wave number ($m$) for each of three chosen Grashof numbers ($\Gr \in \{100, 500, 1000\}$). 
While all $\mu < 0$ lie in the counter-rotation regime and  $\mu > 0$ in the co-rotation regime, we refer to $\mu = -1$ as counter-rotation and $\mu = 1$ as co-rotation in the discussion below.

For the wide--gap case, $\eta=0.6$, the critical Taylor numbers $\Ta_c$ and corresponding critical axial wave numbers ($\kc$) are reported in Table \ref{tbl:eta_0.6_neutral}. 
In all the cases reported the modes at the onset of instability were found to be oscillatory as is evident by nonzero critical frequency $\sigma_{ic}$. 

We start by discussing the fastest growing mode for each of the three values of $\Gr \in \{100,500,1000 \}$.
To this effect, we plot eigenfunctions only for $\mu=-1$ (counter-rotating cylinders) and the largest positive $\mu$ at which we could find instability in the co-rotating regime for each $\Gr$.
Figure \ref{fig:most_unstable_eta_0.6_Gr_100_counterrot} plots the radial variation of critical eigenmodes (top row) and visualizes the normalized critical eigenfunctions (bottom row) for the counter-rotation case with $\mu=-1$ at $\Gr=100$ for which the fastest growing mode is $m=2$. 
The $1d$ radial eigenmodes are defined up to a phase. Here, we choose the phase of eigenmode of the azimuthal velocity perturbation $v$ to be zero. 
This choice is reflected in the fact that radial eigenmodes for $v$ are purely real (see \citet{oishi2021eigentools} for more details).
Slices of the full $3D$ normalized eigenfunctions are constructed using critical radial eigenmodes and corresponding azimuthal and axial wavenumbers. 
The height of the periodic domain $L_z$ can be obtained by
\begin{equation}
L_z = \frac{2\pi}{\kc},    
\end{equation}
with $\kc$ being the corresponding critical axial wavenumber reported in Table \ref{tbl:eta_0.6_neutral}. 
To conserve space, the $r-z$ slices are scaled to a square domain. 
The negative inclination of the spirals with respect to the horizontal is apparent from the contour plots of the azimuthal velocity perturbation in the $r-z$ plane, which is consistent with the expression in Eq. \eqref{eq:perturbation_shape}. 
Furthermore, $m = 2$ mode is visible from the $r-\phi$ slices where contours of $v$ and $T$ can be seen to have two maxima and two minima in the azimuthal $\phi$-direction.   
Figure \ref{fig:most_unstable_eta_0.6_Gr_100_corot} plots the radial variation of critical eigenmodes (top row) and visualizes the normalized critical eigenfunctions (bottom row) for the co-rotation case with $\mu=0.2$ at $\Gr=100$ for which the fastest growing mode is found to be axisymmetric ($m=0$).

For the counter-rotation case of $\mu = -1$, the temperature and azimuthal velocity eigenmodes can be seen to be confined near the inner cylinder for all values of $\Gr$ considered here. At $\Gr = 100$, $\mu \in [-0.2, 0.2]$ axisymmetric toroidal modes are found to be the fastest growing, while for other values of $\mu$, non-axisymmetric helical modes were found to be the most dangerous. 
The critical mode for $\mu = 0.2$ in the co-rotation regime was found to be axisymmetric at $\eta = 0.6$ and $\Gr=100$, which can be seen from the $r-\phi$ slices in Fig. \ref{fig:most_unstable_eta_0.6_Gr_100_corot}. 
We did not find any unstable mode below the Rayleigh-line for $\mu > \eta^{2} = 0.36$ at $\Gr = 100$.

Figures \ref{fig:most_unstable_eta_0.6_Gr_500_counterrot} and \ref{fig:most_unstable_eta_0.6_Gr_500_corot} depict the fastest growing modes for $(\eta,\Gr) = (0.6, 500)$ for counter-rotation $\mu=-1$ and $\mu=0.2$ in the co-rotation regime, respectively. 
The fastest growing mode for $\mu=-1$ is found to be $m=2$. Similar to $\Gr=100$, for the counter-rotation case for $\Gr=500$ the temperature and azimuthal velocity eigenmodes can be seen to be confined near the inner cylinder. 
The critical mode for the $\mu = 0.2$ case was found to be a non-axisymmetric spiral mode with $m = -2$ at $\eta = 0.6$ and $\Gr=500$. 
However, from Fig. \ref{fig:most_unstable_eta_0.6_Gr_500_corot} (top row) and $2D$ slices in Fig. \ref{fig:most_unstable_eta_0.6_Gr_500_corot} (bottom row) it can be seen that unlike the critical eigenmode for the counter-rotation case, this mode is not confined near the inner cylinder. 
No unstable modes were found below the Rayleigh-line for $\mu > \eta^{2} = 0.36$ at $\Gr = 500$.

For $\mu = 0.5$, closed regions of instability are found, see Fig. \ref{fig:neutral_stab_G_1000} (bottom left).

Figure \ref{fig:neutral_stab_eta_0.9_G_1000} shows calculated neutral stability curves for $\mu = \{-1, -0.5, 0.5, 1\}$ at $\Gr = 1000$ for various azimuthal perturbation wavenumbers $m$. 
The critical axial perturbation wavenumber $k_c$ is seen to decrease with increasing $\mu$ in Table \ref{tbl:eta_0.9_neutral}, corresponding to longer wavelength instabilities at the onset. 
We plot neutral stability curves for $\Gr = 1000$ for four selected values of $\mu$ in Fig. \ref{fig:neutral_stab_eta_0.9_G_1000}. 
We select this case because we find unstable modes in the case of co-rotating cylinders ($\mu=1$). 
As shown in Fig. \ref{fig:neutral_stab_eta_0.9_G_1000}, we find unstable modes in the Rayleigh-stable regime at $\Gr = 1000, \mu = 1$ at higher azimuthal wavenumbers, beyond $m = -15$.

Azimuthal mode with $m = -20$ was found to be the fastest growing, with $m = -18, -19, -21$ being close to the onset of $m = -20$, indicating a possibility of mode competition in the weakly nonlinear regime.
