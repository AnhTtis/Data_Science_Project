  \caption{\label{fig:ex:limit:collapse:23}%
    Two collapsed versions $\aonecharti{1}$ and $\aonecharti{2}$ of the 
    \protect\LLEEonechart\ in Figure~\ref{fig:ex:limit:collapse:1}
    that arise by connecting through $\bverti{1}$ to $\bverti{2}$, and $\bverti{2}$ to $\bverti{1}$, respectively. 
    Neither of the \protect\onecharts\ $\aonecharti{1}$ and $\aonecharti{2}$ contains distinct \protect\onebisimilar\ vertices.
    But each of them contains still a \protect\onetransition. So $\aonecharti{1}$ and $\aonecharti{2}$
    are different from their \protect\onefree\ bisimulation collapses~$\aonecharti{10}$, and $\aonecharti{20}$, see Figure~\ref{fig:ex:limit:collapse:45}.
    $\aonecharti{1}$ and $\aonecharti{2}$ are \protect\onechart\ representations of $\aonecharti{10}$ and $\aonecharti{20}$ in Figure~\ref{fig:ex:limit:collapse:45}.
    Neither of $\aonecharti{1}$ and $\aonecharti{2}$ satisfies \LEE\ nor has a \protect\LLEEwitness: 
    the remaining \protect\loopentrytransitions\ are kept in color; 
    they constitute all transitions that are \protect\loopentrytransitions\ in these charts, respectively.
