\subsection{Quantitative and Qualitative Results}
Both quantitative and qualitative scores were used in order to measure the performance of our model.
\begin{table}[h!] 
\renewcommand\arraystretch{1.2}
\scriptsize
\label{tab:different_nets}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|}
\hline

Method&Low Resolution&High Resolution (CycleGAN)\\\hline

PIQUE ($\downarrow$)  & $46.3740$ & $\mathbf{34.1271}$\\ 
MES ($\uparrow$) & $25.5400$ & $\mathbf{38.3202}$\\\hline

\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\caption{Quantitative scores for the proposed model, where lower PIQUE and higher MES correspond to better scores respectively.}
\label{tab:quan_scores}
\end{table}

\caption{(Higher values indicate a better score) Question 1: ``How would you rank the overall image sharpness?", Question 2: ``How would you rank the image artefacts?" }
\label{tab:qual_scores}

PIQUE analysis of the data in Table \ref{tab:quan_scores} showed the original data quality as ``fair" ($46.3740$), which was improved after super-resolution to ``good" ($34.1271$) \cite{piqe_relative_score}. ES also improved after application of the CycleGAN super-resolution. Qualitative scores in Table \ref{tab:qual_scores} also demonstrated a significant improvement in overall image sharpness, however with more image artefacts - this can be seen visually in Figure \ref{fig:output_ims_saggital}.
